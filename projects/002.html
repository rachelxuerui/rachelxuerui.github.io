    Apartment in Gramercy I

There is a scene in Succession S2E2, where Cousin Greg, apartment hunting, tours a small apartment. While he struggles to maneuver around the unit, the broker is talking about all the storage it offers, to which Greg responds: “the thing I need storage for most urgently of all is me.” This project is more or less that — turning an apartment built for things into one that accommodates a life free of them.
The studio apartment — at just 450SF — originally had 3 conjoined walk-in closets, all roughly the same size. The design primarily optimizes these for specific items and makes room for a sleeping nook, which subsequently frees up the rest of the unit for living and dining spaces.
The sleeping nook and closet area are separated by a glass and theatrical scrim assembly, which visually extends the apartment's depth while adding a bit of material intrigue since the scrim goes from translucent to opaque depending on the lighting condition of each side. It is slightly perverse, which we liked, and way cheaper than putting up drywall, which they loved.
As of , the project is built, and after 5 years of use, recently sold to neighbors who intend to combine the units to accommodate their growing family. We are working on some plans for them, it might turn into something, it might not.
