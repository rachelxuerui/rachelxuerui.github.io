• 013
    Installation in Venice

A project with artist Linda Goode Bryant for the 2026 Venice Biennale.

Still Life is a site-specific, outdoor installation of a working farm that grows, harvests, and distributes food in Venice. It is the first farm on the main island in the city’s history.

We could tell you that the farm is lifted off the ground to create shaded areas for film projections; or improve the ergonomics of farmers; or display the crops at eye-level as if they were art objects; or protect them from flood and rodents. All of those are true and good reasons. But we also simply loved the idea that a farm the size of a tennis court should hover like a magic carpet.

The structure is made of spruce trees harvested, prepared, and cross-laminated into panels in the Dolomites, 2 hours from the site. 22 steel columns, also from the region, carry their loads to the ground. While the installation is designed specifically for Venice, it is engineered for a life after the Biennale. The CLT panels are modular and sized for transport by cargo, and its assembly is entirely mechanically fastened (i.e. no glue). At the end of the show, the structure will be disassembled, flat-packed, shipped to its next destination, and reassembled – like a very big piece of IKEA furniture.

Under the farm, multi-media assemblages made of farm tools and AV equipment new and old broadcast a Venice that Biennale goers almost certainly never experience: footages of the Biennale grounds overgrown with weeds (and their removal before opening), ivy-clad buildings at the furthest reaches of the exhibition sites, sounds of a Venice before tourists –– of songbirds, olive trees, still waters…

Installation Design by 000. Structural Engineering by Guy Nordenson and Associates, with local engineering support by Zero 4 Uno.

As of >, the project is on-view at the Venice Biennale, which runs from May 9 - November 21, 2026.
