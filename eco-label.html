Infinite Goods
Redesigning an eco-label to better convey information about brand sustainability


Duration
2 weeks
Role
Product designer
Skills
Figma
Background
Infinite Goods is a startup company that was supported by the Columbia Build Lab and the Columbia Business School. Their mission is to consolidate sustainable brands into one e-commerce platform, and create a standard measuring the sustainability of brands.
Original design
Problem
According to user research, our users did not find the original eco-label to be helpful.
Solution
Redesign the eco-label according to our user needs by adding descriptive text, eliminating the nutrition label design, and designing it in accordance to our style guide.
Designs
Initial designs
These designs were presented to users during an A/B testing session, and all users interviewed preferred the design on the left.
With this preference in mind, I wondered, do we need to keep the style of a label?
Final designs
In the final design, I chose to eliminate the style of a label, opting for a more dynamic design that fits more naturally with the PDP. To further reduce space, I broke down this design into two tabs - People and Planet.
In another A/B test, users preferred this design over the initial redesign. This version of our eco-label is now deployed and active at infinitegoods.eco.
