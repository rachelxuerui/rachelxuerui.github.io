How might we integrate a personalized feed and notifications?

Project overview
Lucy Chen, the CEO of The Bulletin, asked Freya Buison and I to implement updates to The Bulletin app.

We wanted a place for students to see their saved events and applications, and notifications.

However, in developing these pages, we refined and redesigned the overarching user flow and app structure, improving the overall user experience.

What I learned
01
Communication with devs
Since this was a pretty large overhaul of The Bulletin app, Freya and I set up multiple meetings with Matthew Lou, the developer for this project, in order to smooth out any confusion and go over design feasibility. I learned to empathize, and realize that no matter how beautiful your design is, it may be useless if it cannot be realistically implemented.
02
Empty states
We had to consider a lot of potential empty states (ex. what if a user hasn't saved any events?) and design them accordingly. They also had to be designed in such a way that it encouraged users to fill the empty state (ex. save an event!)
03
Consistency
Before Freya and I joined The Bulletin, there had been no designers. Thus, there were no style guides, no components, and a lack of consistency across styles and colors. We tried our best to solve this issue in the redesign, and create consistency across color usage, button usage, etc., and document these rules.
