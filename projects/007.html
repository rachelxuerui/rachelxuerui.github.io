    Townhouse in Tribeca I
A project with all the things you would expect from a townhouse in this zip code: There is a great room for entertaining that leads to a sky-lit chef's kitchen; directly above, there is another kitchen — differently but no less refined — for the family. There are sculptural oak-clad staircases connecting the floors, and an elevator for when the stairs are too much; There is a listening room, a sauna, and an oversized smart-glass floor that brings light to the cellar. There are six bathrooms — one hidden behind a large curved door for guests, two with continuous resin floor-wall-ceiling treatments for the kids; one with meticulously aligned hand-made tiles for the couple…

It's a very nice house. But what we are prouder of still are the speed and cost of the project's delivery. Gut renovating a townhouse of this scale — 6,500 SF, 5 floors, all new MEP systems, elevator, sprinklers — typically takes 3-4 years from design to completion, and starts at $1000/sf. We did it in 20 months at about ⅔ the price.

Part of the trick was logistical: we fast-tracked the LPC & DOB reviews to get the contractor on site while we were still designing. Much of it was design: we prioritized working with existing conditions to expose and reclad, rather than to demolish and rebuild. When we found brick arches hidden behind drywall during demo, we killed the original feature wall and kept the brickwork and equipment exposed (but gold-leafed) instead. The stairs took on a Gehry-isk heft mostly because it was cheaper and faster to clad over the previous finish stair. It's a thousand decisions like these, big and small, that notched the schedule down a day at a time. It didn't hurt that the contractor was terrible at keeping track of change orders.

As of , the project is completed after 16 months of construction. Putting together a press kit for our publicist, between other project demands, may well end up taking longer than construction itself.
