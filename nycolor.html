Overview

NYColor was designed for a semester project in Human Centered Design at Columbia University. The project asks students to improve the lives of workers in NYC through design. This project was completed in collaboration with Johanna De La Rosa, Heather Chevlin, and Jan Kaetker.

Methods

Our initial user research was two-fold: primary research through 6 interviews and secondary research online and with the use of AI tools (ChatGPT, MegaQ).

Secondary research

Through our secondary research, we found that websites with NYC activities have different ideas of what “affordable” or “cheap” means, with prices ranging between $20-80, with access to many activities only available to a select group of people, geographically.
Furthermore, the cost of living in New York City is ~53.9% higher than the national average, indicating more difficulty in finding affordable activities / spending more on entertainment.

NYColor

New York is the greatest city in the world - however, tight budgets and a lack of self-motivation can prevent New Yorkers from exploring the seemingly infinite offerings of the city.

Our solution to this problem is NYColor – the ultimate app for urban exploration. In this gamified approach to city exploration, users can discover hidden gems while earning rewards as they explore the 5 boroughs.

Users can visually keep track of their progress as they explore different neighborhoods, save interesting locations, and color in a map of New York City by foot.

Description
This app aims to motivate users to explore their city on foot, finding new neighborhoods and locations (businesses, parks, public spaces, etc) they would not find otherwise. We aim to motivate users through the following methods: gamification, competition, and rewards.

Our interactive interface allows users to color in a map of NYC (in a color of their choice) and is designed to encourage users to walk more and explore different areas to color in the map.

Through NYColor’s weekly challenges and leaderboard, users are incentivised to walk and explore more to compete with their friends, and in pairing rewards offered by businesses with users, these rewards motivate users to explore more of the city in order to redeem rewards. This way, they are also exposed to businesses they may have not known of previously.

Businesses are able to advertise for no additional cost beyond the rewards they choose to offer. Users who complete their weekly challenge can redeem these rewards, providing businesses with more customers, and potentially more returning customers if the user takes interest in the business.
Finally, during a user’s exploration, they may stumble across interesting locations/businesses. This provides them with knowledge of a location they enjoy that they may not have discovered otherwise, and it provides businesses with more potential customers.
