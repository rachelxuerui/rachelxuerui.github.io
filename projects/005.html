    Project EATS

An eatery and hydroponic grow space for a local non-profit.

The project is many things: an extension of Project EAT's outdoor farm 2 blocks away, an experiment in all-season growing, an eatery serving a neighborhood with few dine-in options, and a multi-function space for community programs.

The site is a storefront in a new residential building — 2,500SF, opens to Livonia Ave, with a stepping tetris-like plan. We laid out the program in bands: the eatery runs the length of the space fronting the avenue; behind it, a semi-glazed enclosure protects arrays of hydroponic grow racks, allowing glimpses into the grow-light-magenta tinted space; further back, a multi-function space accommodates changing uses — eatery seating during food service hours, film screening at night, community workshops on weekends.

The materials are fun, sustainable, and cheap. A seamless linoleum floor in bright yellow (not your grandmother's 9x9 tiles) denote areas for public use. Cork lines the grow space enclosure. Aluminum-clad panels, hung at an angle, bring down the vast ceiling height and reflect scenes from both the street or the grow space. Countertops are made of recycled glass.

As of , the project is sadly on-hold pending changing priorities of the organization. In the meantime, we are working on various projects with Project EATS and Linda on other fronts.
