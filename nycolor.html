NYColor
Gamifying exploration in New York City


Duration
14 weeks
Team
Rachel Michelson,  Johanna De La Rosa,  Heather Chevlin,  Jan Kaetker
Role
Product designer
Skills
Figma, user research
Background
This project was completed for Human Centered Design, taught by Harry West.
Problem
New York is the greatest city in the world - however, tight budgets and a lack of self-motivation can prevent New Yorkers from exploring the seemingly infinite offerings of the city.
Solution
Gamify urban exploration by allowing users to color in a virtual map of New York City, pin interesting locations, compete in weekly challenges, and earn rewards from participating businesses.
User research
User interviews & findings
We conducted 4 open-ended interviews with workers in New York City. These were workers of varying backgrounds, unaffiliated with Columbia University.
Initial designs
Low fidelity
Final designs
Exploration
Ability to color in New York City by walking, pinning interesting locations, and earning rewards.
Rewards system
Since the users we talked to find it hard to participate in activities without spending too much money, we implemented a rewards system enabling users to spend their free time exploring the city while being able to receive discounts at businesses they may not have known about previously.
Prototype
Based on a class vote, our project won "most New York".
