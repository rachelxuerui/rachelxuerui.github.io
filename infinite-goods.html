Overview

Infinite Goods is an eco-friendly e-commerce platform supported by the Columbia Build Lab, curating sustainable brands for customers, rating these brands on their sustainability, and educating consumers on sustainability and sustainable fashion in one platform.

As a product designer for Infinite Goods, I am responsible for the design of the website, including new pages and features.


Process



Homepage

My first job as product designer was to design a homepage for the website.

I was given the following constraints:
  • Eye grabbing splash
  • Low load time
  • Must be possible to implement using basic Shopify template (this was before we had software engineers on our team)
You can see the finalized homepage design here.


Favorites

One part of our MVP is an option for users to add items to a wishlist. However, the basic Shopify template that we use does not have this functionality. Thus, I researched several free Shopify apps, comparing their functionalities and capacity for design changes. I settled on the Shopify app Wishify, which had a nice wishlist page that fit our style guide and flexibility in button design.

However, Wishify was slightly glitchy, and did not display an option for users to see their wishlist from any page. Thus, I mocked up a simple design for this functionality, which Kimly Tor implemented into the website using Liquid.


Favorites on product page, Figma mockup


You can see this implemented design on our website. I recommend going to a product page (ex. here) to check out the functionality. The Wishify app is a bit buggy, but we hope to improve this function when we have more users and more funding.

The next step would be to implement an option for users to add to products to their favorites dirctly from the collection page, as shown below.


Favorites on collection page, Figma mockup


Methodology

As a company with a primary goal of scoring our brands based on their sustainability, we also wish to be transparent with our consumers on how we develop our sustianability scores. Thus, I took on the project of designing our Methodology Page.

I initially designed it to be a long page walking users through how each category is ranked, shown below.


Methodology page V1, Figma mockup


However, seeing as the Sustainability Score has 13 categories, this would be a very long and monotonous page that users would be subjected to scroll through. Thus, I opted for a design that allowed users to explore the categories on the Sustainbility Score label, directly interacting with the label. Clicking on any category would lead to a popup with more information on how that category is scored.


Methodology page V2 (final), Figma mockup


This design is currently being implemented into the website by Surya Akella.


Who we are

My current design project is to design a “Who we are” page, showcasing our team and prior relevant experience. I had a few iterations for this design, which I presented to our team for feedback.


Who we are page, V1 and V2 (from left to right), Figma mockup


After presenting these three designs to the full team, I received feedback that they liked V1 for it’s larger pictures, and V2 for its clean and minimalistic design. I thus used this feedback to synthesize the larger pictures and clean design for V3, which became the final design.
