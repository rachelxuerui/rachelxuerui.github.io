    Apartment on the UWS

A project for an artist. At the heart of the 1,600 SF apartment is the artist's home office. Rather than a static space (the artist loathes perfect rectangles), we propose a pivoting enclosure that opens to create the office, and dynamically reconfigures the (otherwise rectangular) spaces with its deployment. At the end of the work day, the enclosure nests away to make room for other parts of the apartment. A room that exists only when it is used.


Around this centerpiece, we are updating the kitchen using stone remnants, creating a budget Borromini forced perspective in the bathroom with off-the-shelf tiles, and reworking the living room to display more of the artist's personal collection.


As of , construction documentation is complete and we are reviewing bids, sourcing stone, and looking for someone to adopt the 500-lb navy blue cast-iron AGA oven.


Designed in collaboration with Benjamin Vanmuysen.
