RoarReg
Streamlining the course registration process using Google technologies
Project overview
This project was done during a design challenge hosted by Design at Columbia in partnership with Google UX Designers.

For this challenge, students had to identify a problem in Columbia student life and address it creatively using Google technologies.

How might we use Google technologies to improve Columbia student life?
Problem
The current registration process for Columbia University students is an extremely frustrating process, requiring students to cross-reference multiple platforms in order to successfully create a schedule.
SSOL, method for registration
Vergil, method for mock scheduling

Google Calendar, the preferred method of scheduling for students
An example of a major bulletin, with requirements for the Applications Track in the Computer Science major for the School of Engineering an Applied Science
Research
We conducted 30 minute user interview sessions with 3 Columbia University students.
Students have specific preferences for when they take their courses
Students have to cross reference bulletins for degree requirements, their calendars for availabilities, Vergil for mock schedules, and SSOL for registration
The existing process for scheduling is extremely frustrating and emotional
and published a Google Survey, receiving 25 responses
Initial designs
Our approach to addressing this problem centers around the student's schedule and requirements, automating their course registration process and syncing with their existing calendar.
Automated course generation based on a student's existing schedule and preferences
Course information specific to the student, including what degree requirements it fulfills
Consolidated dashboard with recommended courses based on student degree requirements, medium fidelity
Automated course generation, medium fidelity
Final designs
Student dashboard
A consolidated place with relevant registration information, such as a students degree, degree completion status, current schedule, and saved courses.
Search
Ability to search for classes based on what requirements they fulfill, along with other useful attributes.
Schedule generation
Students can generate potential schedules based on their availabilities, preferred availabilities, saved courses, and other criteria. They can select from recommended schedules and modify as they wish.
