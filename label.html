Eco-label, redesigned
Redesigning a scoring system for brand and product sustainability
This project was completed for Infinite Goods, a startup company that was supported by the Columbia Build Lab and the Columbia Business School.

Their mission is to consolidate sustainable brands into one e-commerce platform, and create a universal standard for measuring the sustainability of brands.

PROBLEM
According to user research, our users did not find the original eco-label to be helpful. There is insufficient detail and inconsistency with the overall style of the website. Furthermore, our users have a negative association with nutrition labels.
Original eco-label, designed by Aileen Lee
INSPIRATION & RESEARCH
I looked to similar ranking systems, such as goodonyou, for inspiration, analyzing what about these existing systems is successful.
INITIAL ITERATIONS
I kept three main things in mind as I redesigned the eco-label: consistency with our style guide, an informative visual component, and descriptive text.
FINAL DESIGNS
The new eco-label won a series of A/B tests, and is live on infinitegoods.eco.
A two-tab format, with bars indicating fulfillment of our criteria with descriptive text
