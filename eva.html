Eva
Helping consumers confidently make purchase decisions with an AI powered chatbot
Project overview
Eva was my primary project for my Product Design Internship with ExpertVoice, an e-commerce platform connecting brands and deals with experts.

I was tasked with the responsibility of conducting user research to identify a problem that our users have, and proposing a solution to that problem through design.

Throughout the project, I had the opportunity to present my progress, findings, and designs to the ExpertVoice executive team, and incredibly exciting opportunity.

How might we combine brand and expert content into an experience that helps people find and learn about the best products?
Problem
Recent research indicates that most users come to ExpertVoice with an openness to shop for new products.

It is difficult to make decisions between many products when there is a large amount of information that must be sifted through.



This phenomenon is fairly common, and more well known as the Paradox of Choice. This response stood out to me, and thinking about current advancements in generative AI, I wondered:

what if this purchase decision was made by a generative AI tool, using our valued Expert reviews and product information?

Research
I conducted 30 minute user interview sessions with 5 ExpertVoice users.
Users often have to go to an external website for complete, detailed product information
Users are curious and excited about AI, but have some level of skepticism in its ability and personal data usage
Reviews are used as a tool for purchase decisions, but incomplete ones are taken with a grain of salt
Consumers have different preferences in fit, and need information on how certain products fit


Initial designs
A chatbot interface providing users with product recommendations using Expert reviews, product information, and brand content.

I tested these initial designs on 5 ExpertVoice users in a series of usability tests.
Introduction

The Yeti is intimidating, offputting, and unwelcoming
Grayed out button style does not seem clickable
Too many slides in the introduction - overwhelming
Users like knowing about data usage and privacy
Product suggestions
How much shopping do we want users to do within the chatbot interface?
What primary actions would users find the most useful?
Relevant reviews

Chatbot Interface

Final designs
Search integration

Recent data collected using Scuba and hotjar revealed that search is the primary action from the main navigation page.

Thus, I decided to integrate the primary call to action to access Eva with the search bar, and add potential secondary calls to action in place where users are likely to feel stuck while shopping.
Introducing Eva


An introduction to Eva, designed to provide transparency about AI and data usage, as well as inform users of its purpose.
Suggestion prompts

Quick primary actions with example questions, in ordered help users understand Eva's purpose and functionality
Product suggestions

Utilize Expert reviews and product information to provide Experts with valuable product recommendations, weighing more complete reviews more heavily.

Provide users with the ability to see relevant reviews, providing context and credibility for the product suggesitons.
Supporting Expert reviews

Provide quotes and links to relevant reviews to promote trust in product suggestions
Feedback mechanisms

Provide method of improving the chatbot through user feedback.

Even though, by using an API, we won't be able to necessarily "train" the model, we can still use these feedback tools to gauge success and failure, and adjust inputs and other factors accordingly.
