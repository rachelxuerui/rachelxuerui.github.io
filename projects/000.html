is the origin point of any 3-dimensional object, the basis of an indexing system, the standard precision tolerance, and an architectural practice based in Manhattan. It means many things; it means nothing.

The practice is a certified Minority Business Enterprise in New York City, and was founded in 2023 by Jedidiah Lau, AIA. Current work includes projects with artists and non-profits, private residences, as well as speculative proposals for a local developer.

Prior to independent practice, Jedy was an Associate at Diller Scofidio + Renfro in New York, and a Project Architect at OMA / Rem Koolhaas in Rotterdam and Hong Kong. Design credits include Fondazione Prada in Milan, V&A East in London, Pina Bausch Zentrum in Wuppertal, WKCD in Hong Kong, as well as exhibition designs at the Gaggosian and MoMA. He has published in the New York Review of Architecture, Pidgin, Rumor, and PLAT, and holds an M.Arch from Princeton University, where he received the Suzanne Kolarik Underwood Prize.
